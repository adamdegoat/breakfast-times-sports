Coaching Corner

Five coaching insights from across the game · 1 June 2026

⚡ ATTACK

Build Width Early with Stagger Alignment

In your next training run, set your backline 5-6 metres deeper than normal at the ruck and demand wide receivers pre-align in a 'stagger' — each player one step deeper than the inside man. This gives your 10 a genuine flat-pass option while creating natural depth for the outside backs. Drill it with a 3v2 overlap exercise starting from a pod ruck, insisting the 10 scans before choosing.

🏉 SET-PIECE

Dominate the Scrum with Shoulder Sequencing

A common scrum leak is props engaging shoulders before hips are loaded — cue your loosehead and tighthead to 'hips first, then drive' on every rep in the gym and on the machine. Run a 'shadow scrum' exercise without opposition where the front row holds the bind position for 5 seconds under load, reinforcing body shape before any contest. Reward stillness and posture, not just push.

🛡 DEFENCE

Pressure the 10 with a Shooting Blitz Trigger

Install a visual trigger — when the opposition 9 looks to their left (your right), your blindside winger shoots the 10 line on a 45-degree angle. Practise this decision-making with a walk-through shadow drill before live pressure. The key coaching point is the blitzer must not go too early: they shoot only once the 9's hands move to pass, eliminating the straightforward chip-and-chase counter.

💪 FITNESS

Target Contact Fitness with Ruck Interval Sets

Replace one generic conditioning run with 6 sets of a 'contact circuit': 30m sprint, one dominant tackle hit on a pad, two rucks over a bag, sprint back — 40 seconds on, 30 seconds off. This replicates the explosive-recovery demand of a tight phase game far better than shuttle runs. Track heart rate recovery between sets; aim for players to be back below 75% max before each rep to build genuine aerobic base.

🧠 GAME MANAGEMENT

Use the Referee as a Tactical Clock Reset

Coach your captain and 10 to call a quick word to the referee after any penalty award before the kick is taken — not to dispute, but to confirm the mark and collect the ball slowly. This legitimate 15-20 second pause resets your defensive line and allows tired forwards to reorganise. Pair this with a hand signal system so the wider players know immediately whether you are kicking for touch, taking a tap, or going for goal.
