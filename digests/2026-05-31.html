Sport Science & S&C

Performance insights delivered without mercy

🔬SPORT SCIENCE · NEUROSCIENCE

Lift Heavy First. Then Actually Be Explosive.

Post-activation potentiation — PAP — is what happens when near-maximal contractions trigger phosphorylation of myosin regulatory light chains, leaving the contractile machinery hypersensitised to calcium for the following 3–8 minutes. Rate of force development goes up. Peak power goes up. The whole system is briefly, measurably, better — studies consistently show 2–6% improvements in jump height and sprint output, which sounds modest until you remember that sport is decided in centimetres and milliseconds.

On-field application

Hit a heavy back squat — 85–90% of max — rest precisely four minutes, then execute your lineout jump, tackle drive, or acceleration work. The barbell primed the system. Do not waste the window.

🏋️S&C · POWER

The Deadlift That Actually Makes You Fast

Protocol 5×3 @ 30–40% 1RM trap bar deadlift, maximal intent each rep, 3 min full rest between sets

Load the bar embarrassingly light. Then attack the floor with every available unit of aggression, leaving the ground completely on every rep — if you are not actually airborne, you are doing a slow deadlift and wasting everyone's time. The cue is 'push the earth away', because this is a speed exercise wearing strength-exercise clothing. Land quietly, reset to the starting position with genuine care, and repeat. Three reps of absolute violence per set, not three reps of vague effort.

Rugby transfer

Directly trains the rapid force expression — peak power in under 300 milliseconds — demanded by a scrum engagement, lineout jump, or the first two steps of a tackle.

🌙RECOVERY · CONTRAST WATER THERAPY

Hot. Cold. Horrible. Necessary. Again.

Protocol 3 complete cycles of 2 minutes at 38–40°C followed immediately by 1 minute at 10–15°C, finishing cold. Start within 30 minutes of the final whistle or session end.

Alternating hot and cold water drives a vascular pump: vessels dilate aggressively in heat, constrict sharply in cold, flushing metabolic waste and blunting the inflammatory cascade that follows repeated collision. The cold phases specifically suppress prostaglandin-mediated inflammation — the kind that accumulates over eighty minutes of someone repeatedly running into you at speed.

For rugby

Non-negotiable for forwards who've spent the afternoon being compressed, and backs who've run channels into people considerably larger than themselves — essentially the entire squad, every single week.

Coaching Corner

Five insights. With pedagogical notes. You're welcome.

⚡ATTACK

Flat Runners Are Killing Your Outside Break

If your centres are running straight into the defensive line like moths into a windscreen, the outside backs will never see ball in space. The fix is brutally simple: the 12 must present on a slight arc, forcing the defender to commit inward before the pass goes wide. Depth is not cowardice. Depth is the difference between a try and a hospital visit.

🎓 Coaching Method

Use a three-cone gate drill — place cones to force the arc physically, because telling grown men to run on a curve apparently requires civil engineering. Game-based learning then embeds it: conditioned game where a try only counts if scored outside the 15-metre line.

🏉SET-PIECE

Your Lineout Caller Is Guessing. Stop It.

A lineout without a genuine coded system is just eight men standing in a field hoping for the best — which is fine at a garden party, catastrophic in a semi-final. The caller must read the jumper numbers, the defensive spacing, and call off a pre-agreed menu of three options maximum. Decision fatigue under pressure is a real thing, and your caller already has enough to worry about given the general state of the lineout.

🎓 Coaching Method

Reciprocal coaching works brilliantly here: pair the caller with the hooker and have them debrief each other after every lineout rep. Two people who have to explain their reasoning to each other tend, eventually, to develop some.

🛡DEFENCE

Blitz Defence Without Drift Is Expensive Nonsense

A blitz line that doesn't account for the wider channels is essentially a door with no wall — impressive until someone walks through the gap on the outside. The 10 sets the line speed, the 12 echoes it, but the 13 must hold fractionally wider to cut off the kick-pass or the crash-ball-then-offload combination that will otherwise score every single time. Aggressive defence is excellent. Aggressive defence with a structural hole in it is just aggression.

🎓 Coaching Method

Socratic questioning mid-session — freeze the drill, point at the gap, and ask the 13 where they were going. The answer is usually illuminating and occasionally philosophical. Command style gets the shape right in training; the questions are what make it stick.

💪FITNESS

Your Players Are Fit For the Wrong Game

Endless steady-state running produces endurance athletes who are spectacular at maintaining a moderate pace — useless in a sport built on explosive five-second efforts followed by ten seconds of chaos. Repeated sprint work with incomplete recovery is the actual physiological demand of rugby: three sets of six 30-metre efforts with 15 seconds rest between sprints, four minutes between sets, twice a week. Everything else is just making the players tired.

🎓 Coaching Method

Game-based conditioning — small-sided games with ball-in-play targets of 80% — replicates the metabolic demand while embedding skills. Players engage more than they do running cones, which is handy, because getting a rugby player to genuinely commit to a fitness session without a ball is like getting a Labrador to ignore a sausage.

🧠GAME MANAGEMENT

Territory Is Won Before the Clock Starts

Teams that kick for territory only when they're under pressure have already lost the game management battle — they're reacting, not dictating. The 10 must establish a territory kicking pattern in the first ten minutes of each half, pinning the opposition inside their own 22 before the game becomes physically intense. Field position is compound interest: invest early, collect late, and watch the opposition's decision-making deteriorate as the fatigue and the field position combine.

🎓 Coaching Method

Use a conditioned game where teams score bonus points for winning territory exchanges — tracked with cones on the touchline — rather than just tries. Guided discovery: players figure out the value of the exit kick without being told, which means they actually believe it, unlike information delivered via a Monday morning PowerPoint.
